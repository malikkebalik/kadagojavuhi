J. Ambrose O'Brien was a Canadian actor who was active in the film industry from 1915 to 1945. He was born in Renfrew, Ontario, Canada on May 27, 1885. He was the son of John O'Brien and Mary Ann O'Brien. O'Brien began his career in the theater, performing in various productions in Canada and the United States. He made his film debut in 1915 with the silent film The Man Who Stayed at Home. He went on to appear in over 100 films, including The Mark of Zorro (1920), The Three Musketeers (1921), The Iron Mask (1929), and The Adventures of Robin Hood (1938). O'Brien was married to actress Mary MacLaren, with whom he had two children. He died on April 15, 1945 in Los Angeles, California.
