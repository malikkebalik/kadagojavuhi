    • What Is Karissa Shannon's Net Worth?

    Karissa Shannon is an American glamour model, reality television personality, and former Playboy Playmate who has a net worth of $2 million. Karissa Shannon rose to fame shoulder-to-shoulder with her twin sister, Kristina Shannon, after they were chosen by Hugh Hefner to be his new girlfriends in 2008. Being Hefner's girlfriends, they got a chance to appear in the E! reality series "The Girls Next Door" in 2009. Their first appearance garnered 2.4 million viewers, making it the show's most-watched season premiere. Karissa and Kristina became Miss July and Miss August 2009 in "Playboy" magazine's combined "summer issue." The two decided to move from Hefner's mansion by January 2010 in order to pursue their careers, and that same year they landed small roles in the film "Somewhere," which was written and directed by Sofia Coppola. Karissa and her actor boyfriend Sam Jones III released a 2011 song titled "Juice and Vodka," and they filmed an adult video together, which was released by Vivid Entertainment under the title "Karissa Shannon Superstar."

    Early Life

    Karissa Shannon was born on October 2, 1989, in Ann Arbor, Michigan. Karissa and her twin sister, Kristina, grew up in Clearwater Beach, Florida, and they attended Largo High School.

    Playboy Career

    In 2008, Karissa and Kristina began a relationship with Hugh Hefner and moved into the Playboy Mansion. They starred in the fifth and sixth seasons of the E! reality series "The Girls Next Door," appearing in 15 episodes. Hefner became aware of the twins after he saw a photo shoot they had done to make ends meet while they were living in a Florida trailer park. Hugh flew them to Los Angeles, where they were picked up in a limo and taken to meet him.

    Karissa said of the meeting:

    "We had just turned 18 and were so naive. I remember Hef being very charming and nice. He was wearing silk pajamas and slippers he always wears with his red robe."

    After that meeting, Karissa and Kristina spent two years living in the Playboy Mansion and were each paid $1,000 per week for their appearances on "The Girls Next Door."

    The twins were named Miss July and Miss August 2009 in "Playboy" magazine's combined "summer issue," and by early 2010, they had left the Playboy Mansion. That year they had small roles as "pole-dancing twins" in the Sofia Coppola drama "Somewhere," which starred Stephen Dorff, Elle Fanning, Michelle Monaghan, and Chris Pontius and was named one of the year's top independent films by the National Board of Review. "Somewhere" also won the Golden Lion award for Best Film In Competition at the 67th Venice International Film Festival.
