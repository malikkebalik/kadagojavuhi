All The Television Actors Who Have Earned $1 Million (Or More) Per Episode

September 25, 2024
blog

Making $1 million (or more) per episode of a television/cable/streaming show used to be a fairly rare occurrence. Not anymore. With A-list film actors making the jump to the small screen, salaries have absolutely skyrocketed. Prior to 2017, only a small handful of actors had ever earned $1 million per episode. In the years that have followed, roughly a dozen new members have joined the $1m club, with several members are well above that mark.

The three main cast members of "The Big Bang Theory" – Jim Parsons, Johnny Galecki, and Kaley Cuoco – all began earning $1 million per episode (roughly $24 million per year before backend syndication royalties) for the final three seasons of their show. That was an enormous  jump from the $350,000 per episode they had been earning.

Impressively, Jennifer Aniston has cracked the $1 million per episode mark on TWO different shows! She first became a member of the $1 million per episode or more club in 2002 for "Friends." Aniston earned her second membership card in 2019, thanks to AppleTV+'s "The Morning Show." She earns a whopping $2 million per episode. Her "The Morning Show" costar Reese Witherspoon also has two entries on this list. She made $1 million or more on both "Big Little Lies" and "The Morning Show."

Who was the first television actor to make $1 million per episode?

Jerry Seinfeld. He earned $1 million per episode in 1997.

Has anyone ever earned $2 million per episode?

Yes! A handful of actors have earned $2 million per episode, including Robert Downey Jr. (The Sympathizer), Reese Witherspoon + Jennifer Aniston (The Morning Show) and John Krasinski (Jack Ryan). However, all of these examples are limited run series (8-10 episodes) AND each actor also served as a producer on the show which comes with an additional salary?

What's the most money earned on a per-episode basis in TV history?

On a dollar for dollar basis, $2 million is the highest per-episode salary anyone has ever earned from a television series as of this writing. But there's a catch!

Back in 2002 Kelsey Grammer earned $1.6 million per episode of "Frasier." When you adjust for inflation, earning $1.6 million in 2002 is the same as earning around $2.66 million per episode today! And then two years later, Ray Romano earned $1.7 million per episode. Earning $1.7 million in 2004 is the same as $2.7 million today. So Ray Romano is the highest paid actor in TV history!

In date descending order, below is our running list of all the actors in TV history who have earned $1 million (or more) per episode of television:

  • Kevin Costner for Yellowstone (2022) – $1 million

For season 5 of "Yellowstone" Kevin Costner earned $1.2 million per episode. There were 8 episodes in the season that aired between November 2022 and January 2023. That worked out to around $9.6 million for the season.

  • Elisabeth Moss for Shining Girls (2022) – $1 million

In 2022 Elisabeth Moss earned $1 million per episode to star in 8 episodes of the Apple TV+ series "Shining Girls."

  • Harrison Ford for 1923 (2022) – $1 million
  • Helen Mirren for 1923 (2022) – $1 million

Harrison Ford and Helen Mirren both earn $1 million per episode for their work in the series "1923" which takes place in the "Yellowstone" universe.

  • John Krasinski for Jack Ryan (2022) – $2 million

Between his base acting salary and producing fee, John Krasinski earned $2 million per episode of "Jack Ryan" season three. There were 8 episodes, so John earned $16 million in total.

  • Jason Sudeikis for Ted Lasso (2022) – $1 million

AppleTV+'s "Ted Lasso" cleaned up at the Emmy Awards several times, earning Best Actor for Jason Sudeikis and best-supporting actor and actress for Brett Goldstein and Hannah Waddingham. The show has gained an audience of passionate fans who turn in each week for their dose of Lasso's kindness. It was recently announced that Sudeikis was getting a raise to $1 million per episode for season three of "Ted Lasso."

  • Chris Pratt for The Terminal List (2022) – $1.4 million

Chris Pratt, like Robert Downey Jr., is a member of the Marvel Cinematic Universe due to his role as Star-Lord. He's appeared in four MCU movies. Now, he's taking his talents to Amazon Prime for the adaptation of the Jack Carr novel "The Terminal List." Pratt earned $1.4 million per episode for his starring role in the series.

  • Jeff Bridges for The Old Man (2022) – $1 million

Jeff Bridges last appeared in a television show 50 years ago. The "Big Lebowski" actor is set to debut in FX for Hulu's "The Old Man" in 2022. He will bring home $1 million per episode for the series based on the 2017 novel of the same name by Thomas Perry. Bridges is also executive producing the upcoming series. Bridges is currently battling lymphoma and we are wishing him a full recovery.

  • Michael Keaton for Dopesick (2021) – $1 million

Not only did Michael Keaton earn an Emmy for his work in the 2021 Hulu miniseries "Dopesick", he also earned $1 million per episode over 8 episodes.

  • Robert Downey Jr. for The Sympathizer (2021) – $2 million

Robert Downey Jr. has made several appearances at the top of the highest-paid film actors list. Now, after officially retiring from his role as Iron Man, Downey is joining the group of A-list film actors who've made the jump to the small screen. He's set to star in the HBO adaptation of Viet Thanh Nguyen's Pulitzer Prize-winning espionage thriller "The Sympathizer." Downey will take home at least $2 million per episode for his co-starring role.

  • Norman Reedus for The Walking Dead (2020) – $1 million

Norman Reedus starred on "The Walking Dead" from 2010 to 2022, appearing in 148 of the show's 177 episodes. At the peak of the series in the show's later seasons, Norman earned $1 million per episode. That worked out to $22-24 million in total earnings per season

  • Jennifer Aniston for The Morning Show (2019) and Friends (2002) – $1 million & $2 million

Jennifer Aniston first made the list of actors making at least $1 million in 2002 with the cast of "Friends." She's now made the list for the second time for her starring role in AppleTV+'s "The Morning Show." Aniston is bringing home $2 million per episode. "The Morning Show" season two recently premiered.

  • Reese Witherspoon for The Morning Show and Big Little Lies (2019) – $1 million & $2 million

Reese Witherspoon made the jump to the small screen in the 2019 adaptation of Liane Moriarty's novel "Big Little Lies" for HBO. She also produced the series through her company Hello Sunshine. Witherspoon took home $1 million for each episode. Witherspoon also stars in "The Morning Show" alongside Jennifer Aniston. Like Aniston, Witherspoon brings home $2 million per episode for the streaming show about the volatile reality of a morning news show.

David Livingston/Getty Images

Nicole Kidman starred alongside Reese Witherspoon in HBO's "Big Little Lies." The Oscar-winning actress made $1 million per episode for her role as battered wife Celeste Wright. Kidman is currently starring in another small screen adaptation of a novel by "Big Little Lies" author Liane Moriarty. She plays Masha on Hulu's "Nine Perfect Strangers."

  • Patricia Heaton for The Middle (2018) – $1 million

In the final season of the sitcom "The Middle", Patricia Heaton earned $1 million per episode. There were 24 episodes in the final season, bringing her total earnings to $24 million.

  • The cast of Game of Thrones (2017) – $1 million

The cast of "Game of Thrones" had an epic eight-season run on HBO and garnered a cult-like following. Peter Dinklage, Nikolaj Coster-Waldau, Lena Headey, Emilia Clarke, and Kit Harrington, who played, respectively, Tyrion Lannister, Jaime Lannister, Cersei Lannister, Daenerys Targaryen, and Jon Snow, each made $1 million per episode for the eighth and final season of the series in 2017-2018.

  • The cast of The Big Bang Theory (2014) – $1 million

The cast of "The Big Bang Theory" spent their 2013 hiatus negotiating enormous raises for their eighth season salaries. Sheldon, Leonard, and Penny received $1 million per episode for the 2014, 2015, and 2016 seasons. In total, the three main cast members earned more than $100 million each from the eighth season to the twelfth and final season of the show. Prior to the jump to the million dollar club, Galecki, Parsons, and Cuoco were earning $350,000 per episode.

Charlie Sheen made $1.8 million for the last season he was on "Two and a Half Men." Of course that was the year of Tiger Blood and Winning and all the things that went along with his epic meltdown. As a result, that season was cut short. Sheen appeared in 16 episodes of the improbably long running sitcom. For his trouble, he earned about $30 million. Sheen formerly held the Guinness World Record for Highest Paid TV Actor Per Episode.

James Gandolfini's salary negotiations dragged on for so long they delayed production and threatened to shut down the HBO hit "The Sopranos."  As the fifth season was about to begin shooting, Gandolfini filed a breach-of-contract lawsuit against HBO for, essentially, not giving him the $1 million per episode he'd been seeking. HBO countered with a $100 million lawsuit and production on the mafia drama was put off for two weeks. Finally, the show's executive producer stepped in and Gandolfini got his $1 million an episode –doubling his salary – provided he stick around for at least two more seasons.

  • Ray Romano for "Everybody Loves Raymond" (2004) – $1.7 million

Ray Romano made an astounding $1.7 million for the final two seasons of the long-running sitcom "Everybody Loves Raymond."  The show centered on Romano and his dysfunctional family and it was a huge hit for CBS for a decade and is making all of its cast tons of money in syndication. Earning $1.7 million in 2004, is the same as earning $2.7 million in today's dollars!

  • The cast of "Friends" (2002) – $1 million

During the early years of "Friends," the actors discovered that they were all being paid different salaries ranging from $20,000 to $40,000 per episode.  Before the third season, the actors banded together and negotiated raises as a group. They were then making $75,000 per episode. Every year Courtenay Cox, Jennifer Aniston, Lisa Kudrow, Matthew Perry, Matt LeBlanc, and David Schwimmer negotiated as one and their salaries increased exponentially until, in the ninth and tenth seasons, each of the six main cast members were earning $1 million per episode.  Over the course of 10 seasons, each cast member of "Friends earned" $88.4 million in salary.

  • Kelsey Grammer for "Frasier" (2002) – $1.6 million

Kelsey Grammer played Dr. Frasier Crane on "Cheers." He was supposed to play that character for just six episodes. Instead, he managed to play the neurotic shrink for 20 years. His "Cheers" spinoff "Frasier" was one of the most successful TV spinoffs in history.  The sitcom ran for 11 seasons, collecting 37 Emmy Awards during its long run.  During the last two seasons of the Seattle-based show, Grammer earned $1.6 million per episode for a total gross of just under $77 million over those two seasons. Earning $1.6 million in 2002 is the same as earning $2.66 million per episode in today's dollars.

  • Tim Allen for "Home Improvement" (1998) and "Last Man Standing" (2021) – $1.25 million

Tim Allen made a staggering $1.25 million per episode for the final season of "Home Improvement." Impressively, the sitcom was Allen's first acting gig. He starred as a former salesman turned host of a home improvement show.  "Home Improvement" ran for eight seasons. Also keep in mind that earning $1.25 million in 1998 is the same as earning $2.3 million in today's dollars!

Then in the final season of his series "Last Man Standing", which ran from 2011 to 2021, Tim once again earned $1.25 million per episode. And while YES that's the same amount he earned from "Home Improvement", earning $1.25 million in 2021 is the same making $680,000 in 1998. So Tim was working at a relative discount!

  • Paul Reiser and Helen Hunt for "Mad About You" (1998) – $1 million

It's hard to remember now, but back in the 90s "Mad About You" was a hot property with great ratings and a devoted fan base.  Helen Hunt won the Emmy for  Best Actress in a Comedy four years in a row (1996-1999). The show's stars, Paul Reiser and Helen Hunt each received $1 million per episode for the eighth and final season of the long-running series. That last season carried a 22 episode order, earning Hunt and Reiser $22 million in one season.

  • Jerry Seinfeld for "Seinfeld" (1997) – $1 million

Way back in 1997, Jerry Seinfeld became the first person to take home $1 million an episode for the final season of "Seinfeld."  This was HUGE news 17 years ago. But Jerry could have earned even more if he had wanted to bring "Seinfeld" back for a 10th season.  Reportedly, the network offered Seinfeld $5 million an episode to do one more season.  Seinfeld is the only actor to appear in every episode of the show. His co-stars – Jason Alexander, Julia Louis-Dreyfus, and Michael Richards, were each paid $500,000 per episode at the end of the series.
