What did Ms. Rachel do Before Launching Her YouTube channel?

Sanford, Maine is where Rachel Griffin grew up after being born in Biddeford, Maine. She attended the University of Southern Maine and Sanford High School, where she studied theatre.

Before launching her YouTube channel, she worked as a music teacher at a public preschool in New York City after receiving a master’s degree in music education from New York University. She will get her second master’s degree in early childhood education in 2023.

She and her husband, Broadway music director and composer Aron Accurso, launched their Ms. Rachel YouTube channel in 2019 in reaction to the paucity of media resources for their son, who had a speech delay and didn’t utter his first word until he was two years old.

She produced the YouTube children’s music series Songs for Littles, which combines traditional children’s songs, such as nursery rhymes with her own compositions for young children.

It began as a live class taught by Griffin-Accurso, with a focus on language development milestones and inclusive subject matter and was influenced by the methods used by her son’s early childhood intervention speech therapist. By the time the COVID-19 epidemic started in 2020, the channel had amassed more than five million members.
